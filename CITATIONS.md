## Data Sources & Citations

### Primary Datasets

**EIA Form 860 — Annual Electric Generator Report**
Used for cooling equipment type, plant location, and generator characteristics (2021–2024).
U.S. Energy Information Administration. *Form EIA-860 Annual Electric Generator Report.* Washington, DC: EIA. Retrieved from https://www.eia.gov/electricity/data/eia860/

**EIA Form 923 — Power Plant Operations Report**
Used for annual water consumption, withdrawal, discharge, and net electricity generation (2021–2024).
U.S. Energy Information Administration. *Form EIA-923 Power Plant Operations Report.* Washington, DC: EIA. Retrieved from https://www.eia.gov/electricity/data/eia923/

**EPA eGRID — Emissions & Generation Resource Integrated Database**
Used for plant capacity (MW), latitude/longitude coordinates, and emissions rates (2021–2023).
U.S. Environmental Protection Agency. *Emissions & Generation Resource Integrated Database (eGRID).* Washington, DC: EPA. Retrieved from https://www.epa.gov/egrid

**NOAA Global Summary of the Year (GSOY), Version 1**
Used for annual mean, maximum, and minimum temperature at nearest weather station to each plant.
Lawrimore, Jay H., Ray, Ron, Applequist, Scott, Korzeniewski, Bryant, and Menne, Matthew J. (2016). *Global Summary of the Year (GSOY), Version 1.* NOAA National Centers for Environmental Information. https://doi.org/10.7289/JWPF-Y430
Data access: https://www.ncei.noaa.gov/access/search/dataset-search?text=GSOY

**WRI Aqueduct 4.0 — Water Risk Atlas**
Used for baseline water stress scores (`bws_raw`) spatially joined to each plant location.
Kuzma, S., M.F.P. Bierkens, S. Lakshman, T. Luo, L. Saccoccia, E.H. Sutanudjaja, and R. Van Beek. 2023. "Aqueduct 4.0: Updated decision-relevant global water risk indicators." Technical Note. Washington, DC: World Resources Institute. https://doi.org/10.46830/writn.23.00061
Data download: https://www.wri.org/data/aqueduct-water-risk-atlas

**PNNL/DOE Building America Climate Zone Shapefiles**
Used for ASHRAE climate zone and moisture regime classification via spatial join to plant coordinates.
Antonopoulos, C., T. Gilbride, E. Margiotta, and C. Kaltreider. 2022. *Guide to Determining Climate Zone by County: Building America and IECC 2021 Updates.* PNNL-33270. Richland, WA: Pacific Northwest National Laboratory. https://doi.org/10.2172/1893981
Shapefiles: https://basc.pnnl.gov/guide-determining-climate-zone-county-data-files

### Academic Literature

**Siddik, Shehabi & Marston (2021) — Benchmark spatial water footprint study**
Referenced as the primary prior work on spatially-detailed water footprints of U.S. data centers; methodology compared against our approach.
Siddik, Md. Abu Bakar, Arman Shehabi, and Landon Marston. (2021). The environmental footprint of data centers in the United States. *Environmental Research Letters,* 16, 064017. https://doi.org/10.1088/1748-9326/abfba1

**Lei, Lu, Shehabi & Masanet (2025) — Key determinants review**
Referenced for the finding that even operator sustainability reports lack determinant-level facility data, confirming the data gap encountered in this project.
Lei, Nuoa, Jun Lu, Arman Shehabi, and Eric Masanet. (2025). The water use of data center workloads: A review and assessment of key determinants. *Resources, Conservation and Recycling,* 219, 108310. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.resconrec.2025.108310
